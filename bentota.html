Benthota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a non-profit organization that was founded in 1981 with the goal of protecting and conserving sea turtles. The hatchery is located in the town of Bentota, on the south coast of Sri Lanka. The hatchery works to protect sea turtles in a number of ways.
First, it collects and incubates sea turtle eggs that have been laid on beaches in the area. The hatchery has a high success rate of hatching sea turtle eggs, and it releases thousands of baby turtles into the ocean each year. Second, the hatchery rehabilitates injured sea turtles. Sea turtles can be injured by fishing nets, boat propellers, and other human activities. The hatchery provides medical care to injured sea turtles and helps them to recover.

Third, the hatchery educates the public about sea turtles.
The hatchery offers tours and educational programs that teach visitors about the importance of sea turtles and the threats they face. The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is an important part of the conservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka. The hatchery has helped to protect thousands of sea turtles and has raised awareness about the importance of these creatures.
If you are visiting Sri Lanka, I encourage you to visit the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. It is a great way to learn about sea turtles and to support the important work that the hatchery is doing to protect these creatures.

Beyond its conservation efforts, Bentota Turtle Hatchery also serves as an educational hub, promoting awareness about sea turtles and their conservation needs. Visitors, both locals and tourists alike, have the opportunity to learn about the life cycle of turtles, the challenges they face, and the importance of preserving their habitats.
The hatchery conducts informative sessions and interactive programs, inspiring a sense of responsibility and fostering a deep connection with these ancient creatures. By instilling a love for marine life in the hearts of people, Bentota Turtle Hatchery seeks to create a generation of passionate environmental stewards.
