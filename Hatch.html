Turtle Hatcheries in Sri Lanka

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka, plays a crucial role in the conservation and protection of sea turtles. This renowned hatchery has become a sanctuary for these magnificent creatures, offering them a safe space to lay their eggs and ensuring the survival of turtle populations.


The hatchery serves as a vital educational center, providing visitors with an opportunity to learn about the various species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, including the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Nestled along the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery stands as a testament to the power of conservation and community engagement. Established with a noble mission to protect and preserve endangered sea turtles, this hatchery has become a beacon of hope for these magnificent creatures and a source of inspiration for environmental sustainability.

Benthota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a non-profit organization that was founded in 1981 with the goal of protecting and conserving sea turtles. The hatchery is located in the town of Bentota, on the south coast of Sri Lanka. The hatchery works to protect sea turtles in a number of ways.
First, it collects and incubates sea turtle eggs that have been laid on beaches in the area. The hatchery has a high success rate of hatching sea turtle eggs, and it releases thousands of baby turtles into the ocean each year. Second, the hatchery rehabilitates injured sea turtles. Sea turtles can be injured by fishing nets, boat propellers, and other human activities. The hatchery provides medical care to injured sea turtles and helps them to recover.
