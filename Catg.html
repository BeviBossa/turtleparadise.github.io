Turtle Catogories In Sri Lanka

Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas)

The Green Turtle is a common species in Sri Lanka and can be found nesting on several beaches along the coast, including Rekawa, Kosgoda, and Pigeon Island. These turtles are known for their herbivorous diet, feeding on seagrasses and algae. The Green Turtle gets its name from the green color of its fat tissue and is characterized by its smooth carapace.

Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta)

Loggerhead Turtles are occasional visitors to Sri Lankan waters, and their nesting sites can be found on beaches such as Rekawa and Kosgoda. These turtles have a large head and strong jaws, allowing them to feed on hard-shelled prey. They have a reddish-brown carapace and play a vital role in maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems.

Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata)

Hawksbill Turtles are endangered and are known for their beautiful shells, which have high commercial value. These turtles can be found in Sri Lanka, particularly in the waters around Pigeon Island and Hikkaduwa. Hawksbill Turtles feed on sponges and other invertebrates, making them important for coral reef health.

Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea)

Olive Ridley Turtles nest in large numbers along Sri Lanka's beaches, including Rekawa and Kosgoda. They are the smallest species of sea turtle and have olive-colored carapaces. These turtles undertake mass nesting events known as "arribadas," where thousands of females come ashore to lay their eggs.
